Part 1 - Data Engineering is Cool, Too

Jonathan Adams and Julian Swart Dec 2022 1 min read

Data can be separated into two stages: processing it for use (data engineering) and using it (data science, analytics, ML, etc.) - each one needing the other. At work, my colleague and I largely stay within the scope of the latter. Lately, however, the less popular but immensely impactful subject of data engineering has been the focus. This collaboration project stemmed from

wanting to deepen our understanding of the entire data process and needing to showcase some of our abilities to the public. Part 1 will display what we learned about data engineering. Part 2 will exhibit some of our data science skills. I hope you enjoy!

Instead of using an online dataset, we decided to simulate what a real company’s data looks like - since this is what we use in our day jobs (but are unable to share since it’s their data). We came up with a fake business called JJ Furnitiure (JJ stands for Jon and Julian) and created artifical transaction, customer, and product data.
